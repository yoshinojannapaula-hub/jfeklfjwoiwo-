About Negros Occidental

Nestled in the Western Visayas, Negros Occidental is known as the "Sugar Bowl of the Philippines" for its vast sugarcane fields. It blends Spanish colonial heritage with vibrant local traditions, offering natural wonders, historic landmarks, and world-class cuisine.

Capital: Bacolod City (The City of Smiles)

Must-Visit Attractions

The Ruins

Called the "Taj Mahal of Negros," this grand mansion ruin glows golden at sunset.

Mount Kanlaon

The highest peak in the Visayas, perfect for trekkers and nature lovers.

Lakawon Island

Pristine white sand beach with Asia's largest floating bar.

Silay Heritage Houses

Over 30 well-preserved ancestral homes showcasing colonial elegance.

Culture & Cuisine

Festivals

  • Masskara Festival (October): Vibrant street dances with colorful smiling masks.
  • Panaad sa Negros (April): Celebrates the province's 32 towns and cities.

Local Delicacies

  • Chicken Inasal: Grilled chicken marinated in local spices.
  • Piaya: Sweet flatbread filled with muscovado sugar.
  • Napoleones: Custard-filled pastry with Spanish influences.

Sustainable Tourism

Negros Occidental leads in eco-friendly travel, with initiatives like organic farming, protected areas (e.g., North Negros Natural Park), and green-certified hotels. San Carlos City is even recognized as an ASEAN Clean Tourist City.
